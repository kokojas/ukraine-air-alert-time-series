At first, I treated the task as a forecasting problem. During EDA, that felt irresponsible: air raid alerts are public-warning events, not direct attack records, and the official dataset changes granularity in late 2025. I adjusted the scope toward alert burden and fatigue, using the stable oblast-level series for duration, nighttime disruption, rolling burden, regional differences, and co-alert similarity. Then I added validation tests for interval merging, local-time splitting, zero-alert days, and the baseline model. The final version is better because it is narrower, reproducible, and honest about uncertainty. The model remains only a benchmark, not a life-safety recommendation.
